Merit-Based Scholarship Programs

Detailed breakdown of India's top government merit-based scholarship schemes and how to apply.

Central Government
₹12,000 – ₹20,000 / year

Central Sector Scheme of Scholarship for College & University Students

One of India's most prestigious central government scholarships, this scheme rewards academically exceptional students from low-income families who are pursuing higher education in degree or professional programs.

📋 Key Eligibility

  • Must be above the 80th percentile of successful candidates in Class XII board exam
  • Annual family income ≤ ₹4,50,000
  • Pursuing a full-time, recognized degree or professional course (B.E., MBBS, B.Sc., etc.)
  • Not availing any other central government scholarship

💰 Award Amount

  • Years 1–3: ₹12,000 per annum
  • Years 4–5: ₹20,000 per annum (Professional courses)
  • P.G. level: ₹20,000 per annum

⏳ Duration

  • Renewable annually for up to 5 years
  • Must maintain 50%+ marks and 75%+ attendance

🚀 How to Apply

1Visit the National Scholarship Portal (NSP) at scholarships.gov.in
2Register as a new applicant and complete your profile
3Search for "Central Sector Scheme" and fill the application form
4Upload required documents (marksheets, income certificate, bank details)
5Submit and track status through NSP dashboard
Apply on NSP →
Central Government (2008)
₹12,000 / year

National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship Scheme (NMMSS)

Launched in 2008, NMMSS aims to reduce dropout rates after Class VIII by providing ₹1,000 per month to meritorious students from economically weaker sections studying in government or government-aided schools.

📋 Key Eligibility

  • Students in Government, Government-aided, or local body schools
  • Secured at least 55% marks (or equivalent) in Class VII (5% relaxation for SC/ST)
  • Parental income from all sources ≤ ₹3,50,000 per annum
  • Must appear in state-level selection exam (MAT + SAT)

💰 Award Amount

  • ₹12,000 per annum (₹1,000/month)
  • Disbursed directly to student's bank account via DBT

⏳ Duration

  • Renewable for 4 years (Class IX to XII)
  • Must maintain 55% grades (50% for SC/ST)

🚀 How to Apply

1Check the state-wise quota and exam dates announced by your state education board
2Register on the National Scholarship Portal (NSP)
3Appear in the MAT (Mental Ability Test) and SAT (Scholastic Aptitude Test)
4Selected students receive provisional letters and must submit documents online
5Scholarship credited annually upon renewal verification
Apply on NSP →
DST – Govt. of India
₹80,000 / year

INSPIRE Scholarship for Higher Education (SHE)

Under DST's INSPIRE programme, the SHE component attracts young talent to pursue fundamental sciences — one of the most generous scholarship awards in India for science students.

📋 Key Eligibility

  • Top 1% in Class XII board exam OR JEE/NEET rank holder
  • Enrolled in B.Sc., B.S., Int. M.Sc., or M.Sc. in Natural/Basic Sciences
  • No age bar — based entirely on merit
  • Must be at an NAAC-accredited or autonomous institute

💰 Award Amount

  • ₹80,000 per annum (₹60,000 scholarship + ₹20,000 mentorship grant)

⏳ Duration

  • Up to 5 years for the full undergraduate programme
  • Annual renewal based on academic performance

🚀 How to Apply

1Visit online-inspire.gov.in and register
2Select "SHE" scholarship and fill in your academic details
3Upload Class XII marksheet, proof of admission & institute details
4Institute verifies and DST releases funds
Apply on INSPIRE →
CBSE
₹500 / month

CBSE Merit Scholarship for Single Girl Child

Recognizing and supporting single girl children of their parents, this CBSE scholarship encourages girl education at the senior secondary level in CBSE-affiliated schools.

📋 Key Eligibility

  • Must be a single girl child of her parents (no siblings)
  • Secured 60%+ marks in Class X CBSE board exam
  • Continuing studies in Class XI and XII in a CBSE-affiliated school
  • School tuition fee not exceeding ₹1,500/month

💰 Award Amount

  • ₹500 per month (₹6,000 per year)

⏳ Duration

  • 2 years — Class XI & XII only
  • Discontinued if student transfers to a non-CBSE school

🚀 How to Apply

1Visit cbse.gov.in and navigate to the Scholarship section
2Fill the online application form with personal and academic details
3Submit an affidavit confirming single girl child status
4School principal verification and CBSE approval
Apply on CBSE →
West Bengal Govt.
₹1,000–₹8,000 / month

Swami Vivekananda Merit Cum Means Scholarship (SVMCM)

The West Bengal government's flagship scholarship, SVMCM provides financial assistance to bright but economically challenged students from Class IX through postgraduate level, across engineering, medical, general degree programmes and more.

📋 Key Eligibility

  • Permanent resident of West Bengal
  • Annual family income ≤ ₹2,50,000
  • Achieved minimum 60% marks in last qualifying exam
  • Enrolled in recognized WB institution

💰 Award Amount (by course)

  • Class IX–XII: ₹1,000/month
  • UG (Arts/Science/Commerce): ₹1,000/month
  • UG Engineering/Medical: ₹5,000/month
  • PG (General): ₹2,000/month
  • PG (Engineering/Medical): ₹8,000/month

🚀 How to Apply

1Visit svmcm.wbhed.gov.in
2Register with Aadhaar-linked mobile number
3Fill in academic and income details, upload documents
4Institute verifies application
5Scholarship disbursed to bank account via DBT
Apply on SVMCM →
